Refund Policy

Last Updated: June 1, 2025

Important: All ticket sales are final unless the event is canceled, rescheduled, or as required by law. Please review our full policy below.

1. General Policy

All ticket purchases through ConcertTix are typically final and non-refundable. Exceptions may apply in the following circumstances:

  • Event cancellation
  • Event rescheduling
  • Significant event changes
  • As required by applicable law

2. Event Cancellations

If an event is canceled without being rescheduled, we will automatically issue refunds to the original payment method within 14 business days. No action is required from you.

3. Rescheduled Events

If an event is rescheduled:

  • Your tickets will typically be valid for the new date
  • If you cannot attend the new date, refunds may be available within 30 days of the reschedule announcement
  • You must request a refund during the specified window

4. Refund Processing

4.1 Timeframe

Refunds typically process within 7-14 business days after approval. The time to appear in your account depends on your financial institution.

4.2 Refund Amount

Refunds include the ticket face value and any service fees. Delivery fees are non-refundable unless the entire order is canceled.

5. Non-Refundable Situations

We generally cannot provide refunds for:

  • Change of mind or personal circumstances
  • Late arrival or failure to attend
  • Partial event cancellations (e.g., one performer cancels in a festival)
  • Venue or performer changes (unless significant)

6. How to Request a Refund

For eligible refunds, please contact our customer service team with your order details. Refund requests must be submitted within the specified timeframe.

7. Disputes and Chargebacks

If you believe you are entitled to a refund, please contact us before initiating a chargeback. Unauthorized chargebacks may result in account suspension.

8. Contact Us

For refund inquiries, please contact our customer service team at support@concerttix.com or through our website contact form.
